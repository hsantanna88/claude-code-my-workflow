## Issues Found

### Issue 1: Tax competition literature entirely absent
**Severity:** MEDIUM | **Deduction:** -3

Wilson (1986), Zodrow & Mieszkowski (1986), and Keen & Konrad (2013 survey) are standard references in any local taxation paper. The domain profile explicitly flags "tax competition" as a key referee concern. If a wealthy mayor suppresses IPTU, do neighboring municipalities respond strategically? This dimension is not addressed anywhere in the review.

### Issue 2: Capitalization literature absent
**Severity:** MEDIUM | **Deduction:** -3

Oates (1969), Brueckner (1979), Palmon & Smith (1998). If IPTU changes capitalize into property values, the wealthy mayor's own wealth changes endogenously in response to their policy. This is both a causal mechanism and an identification threat. The domain profile lists "capitalization" as a primary referee concern.

### Issue 3: Ghost citation -- de Magalhaes et al. (2020)
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -2

The frontier map cites "de Magalhaes et al. (2020)" regarding external validity of close-election RDD for politician characteristics. However, this paper has no annotated entry in the bibliography and no BibTeX entry. Internal consistency error.

### Issue 4: Missing broader conflict-of-interest literature
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -1

Fisman & Wang (2015) on connected Chinese firms, Khwaja & Mian (2005) on political lending. These are canonical "politicians benefiting themselves" papers.

### Issue 5: Missing broader property tax in developing countries
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -1

Bahl & Martinez-Vazquez (2007), Norregaard (2013 IMF WP), Bird & Slack (2004). The review is heavily Brazil-focused. JPubE or JDE reviewers would expect awareness of broader development finance literature.

### Issue 6: Missing empirical Tiebout/sorting papers
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -1

Tiebout (1956) is included as theory only. No empirical sorting papers (Epple & Sieg 1999; Banzhaf & Walsh 2008). The domain profile explicitly flags "Sorting/Tiebout" as the first referee concern.

### Issue 7: Minor proximity score inconsistencies
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -1

Monteiro & Ferraz (2012) is rated Proximity 4 but labeled MEDIUM relevance. Szakonyi (2020) book rated Proximity 5, identical to the 2018 article.

### Issue 8: No Portuguese-language scooping check
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -1

No search in ANPEC proceedings, IPEA Discussion Papers, SciELO Brazil, or FGV working papers.

### Issue 9: JEEA inconsistency
**Severity:** LOW | **Deduction:** -1

JEEA appears in framing options but absent from the ranked journal list.
