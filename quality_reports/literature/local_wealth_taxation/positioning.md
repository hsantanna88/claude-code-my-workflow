## Suggested Contribution Statement

"This paper is the first to study whether the geographic concentration of politicians' personal wealth in their own jurisdiction affects local fiscal policy. Using mandatory asset declarations from Brazilian mayoral candidates and a regression discontinuity design exploiting close elections, we show that mayors with wealth concentrated in their municipality [outcome TBD] relative to mayors whose wealth is geographically dispersed. These findings speak to a 'skin in the game' mechanism in local governance: when politicians' personal assets are directly affected by the policies they set, fiscal outcomes change."

## Key Novelty Dimensions

1. **Geographic concentration of wealth** -- not just total wealth, but whether it is local
2. **Fiscal policy outcomes** -- tax rates, tax revenue, spending composition, cadastre investment
3. **Brazilian municipal setting** -- rich institutional data, validated RDD framework
4. **Skin-in-the-game mechanism** -- separable from self-dealing using heterogeneity tests

---

## Mechanisms to Test

| Mechanism | Prediction | Test |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Skin-in-the-game (positive) | Local-wealth mayors govern better: higher revenue, better services | Effect on overall fiscal performance, service delivery |
| Self-dealing (negative) | Local-wealth mayors suppress property taxes to benefit own real estate | Effect on IPTU rates/revenue specifically |
| Information (positive) | Local-wealth mayors know tax base better: improve collection efficiency | Effect on tax/GDP ratio, compliance rates |
| Selection (confound) | Wealthy candidates who invest locally differ on unobservables | Robustness: control for total wealth, education, occupation |

## Framing Options

### Option A: Political Economy of Taxation
"Who sets property taxes in developing countries? We show that the personal wealth portfolio of local leaders shapes fiscal capacity."
- Audience: Public finance, development economics
- Journals: JPubE, JDE, AEJ:EP

### Option B: Politician Identity and Policy
"Does it matter what politicians own? We extend the politician-characteristics literature to wealth composition and show it affects local fiscal policy."
- Audience: Political economy, applied microeconomics
- Journals: AEJ:Applied, JOP, JEEA

### Option C: Self-Dealing in Local Government
"When the tax collector owns the tax base: Conflicts of interest in local property taxation."
- Audience: Governance, institutional economics
- Journals: JPE, AER, QJE (ambitious)

### Recommended: Option A or B, depending on results
If self-dealing dominates, Option C becomes viable for top-5 journals.
